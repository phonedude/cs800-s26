## Summary

Yao's dissertation argues that large language models, despite their power as text generators, are fragile when deployed in the real world without additional scaffolding. The work builds its case in three acts: first defining the problem through a series of increasingly realistic benchmarks (WebShop, WebArena, SWE-bench), then proposing two reasoning frameworks to close the performance gap (ReAct, Tree of Thoughts), and finally unifying the field under a formal cognitive architecture (CoALA). Taken together, the dissertation established the vocabulary, benchmarks, and methods that define the language agents research area today.
