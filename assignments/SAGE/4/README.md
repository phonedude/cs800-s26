## Overview
This literature review explores the use of artificial intelligence, particularly diffusion-based machine learning models, in weather forecasting. Weather prediction is inherently difficult due to the chaotic nature of atmospheric systems, where small changes in initial conditions can lead to large variations over time. Measurement limitations for temperature, pressure, wind, and moisture contribute to additional uncertainty. The papers reviewed examine AI approaches to address these challenges, focusing on probabilistic forecasting, data assimilation, and deep learning applications for weather modeling.

---

## Papers Reviewed
1. **[Elucidated Rolling Diffusion Models for Probabilistic Forecasting of Complex Dynamics](papers/ERDM.pdf)**
   Introduces a generative framework using diffusion models for probabilistic weather forecasting. Combines a rolling forecast structure with elucidated diffusion models to improve accuracy and uncertainty estimation over time.

2. **[Deep Learning-Based Aerosol and Ocean Data Retrieval from Satellite Polarimeter Measurements](papers/Deep_Learning-Based_Aerosol_and_Ocean_Data_Retrieval_from_Satellite_Polarimeter_Measurements.pdf)**
   Demonstrates how deep learning can replace computationally expensive radiative transfer calculations in satellite data retrieval. A recurrent neural network model achieves high accuracy while reducing computation time from hours to seconds.

3. **[Generative Data Assimilation of Sparse Weather Station Observations at Kilometer Scales](papers/sparse_weather_station_observation.pdf)**
   Proposes a score-based diffusion model for reconstructing high-resolution atmospheric states from sparse weather station data. Improves accuracy over traditional numerical assimilation approaches and efficiently models realistic weather structures.

4. **[DiffDA: A Diffusion Model for Weather-Scale Data Assimilation](papers/diffda.pdf)**
   Uses a denoising diffusion framework in combination with the GraphCast neural weather model to generate initial atmospheric conditions. Achieves high-resolution reconstructions even with sparse observations, demonstrating scalability and accuracy.

5. **[Foundations of Diffusion Models in General State Spaces: A Self-Contained Introduction](papers/Diffusion_Model_Primer.pdf)**
   Provides a comprehensive introduction to diffusion models across continuous and discrete state spaces. Offers foundational understanding of the mathematical principles underlying diffusion-based generative modeling.
